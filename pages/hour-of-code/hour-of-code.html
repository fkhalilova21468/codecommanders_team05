On the 6th of November 2024, our team of 4 ran a coding event ‚Äì "Hour of Code". It took place in secondary school number 240 in Nasimi district. A total of 20 students from the 6th grade attended the event.


ADA - Your Future Starts Here!ü™∂

First, we presented informative slides about our background and Ada University, especially the School of IT and Engineering.

The students did not have a background in coding; however, were very excited to learn about it. After a little theoretical lecture, participants started solving the ‚ÄúMinecraft‚Äù themed coding exercises hosted on the code.org website.During the event, we divided the job into several roles: presenting, technical assistance, and helping students with coding exercises. Through these interactive exercises, students explored programming concepts such as sequencing, control flow, and loops.

Who will be the hero?üí°

To make the learning process even more interactive, we encouraged the most successful students to help their stuck classmates or to explain the solution in front of the whole class.Thanks to this policy, we did not have many students who performed significantly worse than others or were left behind. Also, as team members we were always ready to give hints and assist with any problems. Overall, by the end of the event, people successfully finished approximately 2 3ds of the course, and they were informed that they can later continue coding on the website as they wish. After everyone had a basic understanding of common instructions and how they could be placed in a sequence, nobody had a lot of struggles with any particular coding exercise.

We are Junior Coders! üíª

At the end of the Hour of Code, every participant received a certificate for completing The Houf of Code and demonstrating an understanding of the basic concepts of Computer Science. Students had a feeling of accomplishment since they were working hard for an hour crunching new programming concepts. Despite initial challenges and some technical issues, the students enjoyed the activities, and many showed potential for further exploration in programming. We left the event feeling fulfilled, knowing we had sparked curiosity in the students. After all certificates were handed out, we all gathered to take a group photo. Both participants and organizers received positive emotions and were feeling good about themselves. This experience taught us valuable lessons in planning, problem-solving, and teamwork.
